Recommended Next Steps

This is a diagnostic, not a proposal — Groundworks is already onboarded for outbound, and the items below are the practical next steps this audit points to, not a program pitch. Nothing here has a price tag; sequencing is what matters right now.

1
Ship the SEO Basics

The site itself is in good shape — 90% Site Health, 775ms load time. What's missing is the housekeeping that lets search engines and AI crawlers actually index it.

What needs to happen

  • Add sitemap.xml and robots.txt — neither exists (both 404)
  • Write meta descriptions for all 5 pages — currently 0 of 5 have one
  • Add llms.txt and basic Organization/LocalBusiness schema
None of this touches design or copy — it's pure technical setup, and it's the fastest lever available.
2
Establish Local Presence

No GBP, no LinkedIn company page, no directory citations turned up. The outbound motion is the primary growth engine here, but prospects who get a letter, email, or call will look Groundworks up — there should be something credible to find.

Low-cost, same-week fixes

  • Claim a Google Business Profile for 237 Langley Pl, Woodruff, SC
  • Create a LinkedIn Company Page linking to the site
  • Confirm NAP consistency once these exist
None of this requires the site to be unlocked first.
3
Build the Keyword Baseline

Search volume in this category is small — landscape consulting (210/mo), landscape business consultant (110/mo) — and both are already held by Harvest Landscape Consulting (#2 and #1). The bigger opportunity is Harvest's weak long tail: 79 adjacent keywords worth 44,530/mo sitting at positions 30–94.

Now confirmed on the live site

  • About page exists and tells the owner-operator story — matches what Brian leads with on sales calls
  • Academy page is live — ready for content once the training program launches
  • No dedicated consulting/services page yet, and no blog — the two things a content plan should prioritize first
